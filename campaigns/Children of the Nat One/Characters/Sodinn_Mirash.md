# Narrative Information

## Character Summary

Raised within a volcanic temple, Sodinn was taught that strength was a divine gift and restraint a sacred duty. When monstrous bears destroyed his home and slaughtered his mentors, his faith was transformed into righteous fury. The surviving priests declared his wrath sacred, and from that day forward he became a living instrument of divine judgment.

He does not seek redemption.

He seeks judgment.

Most people instinctively avoid speaking to Sodinn. His immense size, broken horn, volcanic scales, and unwavering stare make him seem like living judgment, though those who know him discover a compassionate heart.

# Additional Notes

- Can hold his breath for approximately fifteen minutes.
- The broken horn is permanent.
- His rage is devotional rather than uncontrolled.
- Uses strength in service of justice.

# Continuity Notes

Sodinn should always be portrayed as a holy warrior whose fury is governed by faith. Gold, crimson, and black dominate his palette, while volcanic light, embers, dragon scales, and his broken horn reinforce his identity as the party's embodiment of righteous judgment.
