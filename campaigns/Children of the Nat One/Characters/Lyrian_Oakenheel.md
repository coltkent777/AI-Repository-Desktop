# Narrative Information

## Character Summary

Lyrian Oakenheel was raised among a secluded tribe of wood elves deep within the Eldwood Forest, where she learned to live in harmony with nature as a hunter, tracker, and protector. After invaders devastated her homeland through fire and deforestation, she left the forest in search of the legendary Lost Glade—a sacred place said to possess the power to heal both the land and the wounds carried by her people. Though vengeance once fueled her journey, her travels continue to teach her that true balance may lie in restoration rather than revenge.

## Personality Traits

- Quiet and observant.
- Deeply connected to nature.
- Fiercely protective of those she loves.
- Prefers solitude or the company of animals over crowded cities.
- Patient, disciplined, and slow to speak.

## Ideals

**Balance.** The wilds are meant to be protected, not conquered.

## Enemies / Dislikes

- Those who destroy nature for greed.
- Those who exploit the wilderness without respect.

# Physical Appearance

Lyrian possesses the quiet elegance common among wood elves. Her sun-kissed olive skin, long copper-raven braid, and striking gold eyes give her an unmistakable beauty, though she rarely draws attention to herself.

She carries herself with calm confidence and deliberate movements, always appearing aware of her surroundings. Whether standing watch or walking through dense forest, she moves with the effortless grace of a seasoned hunter.

# Additional Notes

- Can hold her breath for approximately eight minutes.
- Enjoys quiet moments, exploring untouched wilderness, animals, and the ancient songs of her people.
- Dislikes needless destruction of nature, arrogance, and those who disrespect the wilds.
- Her lifelong goal is to discover the Lost Glade and restore her homeland.
- She hopes to one day rediscover the ancient wisdom once practiced by her people's druids.

# Continuity Notes

Lyrian should consistently be portrayed as calm, observant, and deeply connected to the natural world. Her beauty should feel understated rather than commanding; she does not seek attention and often blends naturally into her surroundings. Her palladium hunting rifle, white wolves, emerald-and-palladium color palette, and woodland symbolism should remain recurring visual elements throughout future artwork.
