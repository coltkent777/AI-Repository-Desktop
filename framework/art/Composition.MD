# Purpose

This document defines the default composition standards for artwork created using the Art Framework.

These standards establish how subjects are framed, positioned, and presented to ensure every image feels like part of the same cohesive collection.

Campaigns may define approved exceptions, but they do not modify these framework standards.

---

# Definitions

## Character Manifestations

Character Manifestations are the visual elements that originate from a character and communicate their identity without relying on traditional environmental scenery.

Character Manifestations may include, but are not limited to:

- Magic
- Weapons
- Familiars
- Animal companions
- Divine energy
- Nature
- Smoke
- Fire
- Ice
- Lightning
- Spell effects
- Particles
- Floating objects
- Shadows
- Spirits
- Symbolic objects
- Other visual elements directly associated with the character

Character Manifestations exist to reinforce the character's identity and should always remain visually subordinate to the character.

# Composition Standards

## Default Orientation

Portrait orientation is the default for all character artwork.

Landscape orientation should only be used when the requested asset naturally requires it, including:

- Group portraits
- Party artwork
- Collector pieces
- Logos
- Other intentionally wide compositions

---

## Camera Position

The default camera angle is **slightly below eye level**.

This creates a subtle heroic perspective while maintaining a natural and believable viewpoint.

Extreme camera angles should be avoided unless explicitly requested.

---

## Character Framing

The default framing is **waist-up**.

This provides sufficient space to showcase:

- Facial expression
- Equipment
- Character Manifestations

without sacrificing readability.

---

## Subject Placement

The primary subject should remain centered within the composition.

The collection should present a consistent collector's edition presentation rather than highly experimental layouts.

---

## Background Philosophy

Backgrounds exist only to support the character.

The default background should be:

- Transparent
- Pure black
- Nearly black

Traditional scenery should generally be avoided.

Avoid using architecture, furniture, walls, landscapes, or decorative environments to define the character.

Instead, the surrounding space should be occupied by the character's own **Character Manifestations**.

The character creates the environment.

The environment should never define the character.

---

## Negative Space

Maintain a moderate amount of negative space.

The composition should feel balanced, readable, and uncluttered.

---

## Visual Hierarchy

The viewer's attention should naturally flow as follows:

1. Face and expression (~60%)
2. Character Manifestations (~40%)

Supporting elements should reinforce this hierarchy rather than compete with it.

---

## Character Pose

Characters should appear calm, confident, and intentional.

Movement should primarily be expressed through Character Manifestations and secondary elements such as:

- Hair
- Fabric
- Smoke
- Particles
- Familiars

rather than exaggerated body motion.

---

## Cropping

Important character elements should remain fully visible whenever possible.

Avoid cropping:

- Primary weapons
- Shields
- Staffs
- Spellbooks
- Character Manifestations
- Significant equipment

Cropping should never obscure information important to understanding the character.

---

## Collection Consistency

Character portraits should maintain a highly consistent compositional language.

The objective is for the collection to feel professionally curated rather than individually interpreted.

Consistency should always take priority over experimentation.

---

# Campaign Exceptions

Campaigns may intentionally deviate from these standards.

Any deviation must be explicitly documented within that campaign's STYLE.md.

Campaign exceptions supplement the framework.

They do not modify the framework itself.
