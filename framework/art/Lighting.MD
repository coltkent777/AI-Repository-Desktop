# Purpose

This document defines the permanent lighting standards for artwork created using the Art Framework.

Lighting exists to improve readability, establish mood, and reinforce the character as the focal point of every composition.

Campaigns may document approved lighting exceptions, but they do not modify these framework standards.

---

# Lighting Standards

## Primary Light Source

Artwork should generally be illuminated by a single dominant light source.

Additional illumination may exist when justified by Character Manifestations, but the overall lighting should remain visually coherent and easy to understand.

---

## Lighting Mood

The default lighting style should feel:

- Dramatic
- Ethereal

Lighting should create atmosphere while maintaining a believable visual hierarchy.

---

## Character-Driven Lighting

Whenever possible, the primary lighting should feel connected to the character.

Character Manifestations may emit light and influence nearby surfaces.

Examples include:

- Spell effects
- Divine energy
- Magical weapons
- Elemental powers
- Glowing runes
- Spirit companions

Lighting should reinforce the character's identity rather than simply illuminate the scene.

---

## Rim Lighting

Rim lighting is not part of the framework's default style.

If edge lighting exists, it should originate naturally from Character Manifestations or the primary light source rather than appear as a separate stylistic effect.

---

## Color Temperature

Lighting color should be determined by the character and the narrative.

Examples include:

- Warm divine light
- Cool moonlight
- Emerald nature magic
- Crimson infernal magic
- Arcane violet energy

Lighting should support the character's identity while remaining visually harmonious.

---

## Shadows

Shadows should remain soft and natural.

They should create depth without obscuring important visual information.

Heavy or crushed shadows should be avoided.

---

## Readability

Readability always takes priority over atmosphere.

Even intentionally dark artwork should remain immediately understandable on a digital display.

The primary subject should never disappear into shadow.

---

## Background

The framework's default background is:

- Transparent
- Pure black
- Nearly black

Background darkness exists to emphasize the character and their Character Manifestations rather than environmental scenery.

---

## Signature Visual Identity

Every artwork should immediately communicate that it belongs to the collection.

The framework achieves this through the consistent use of:

- Black or transparent backgrounds
- Character-driven lighting
- Character Manifestations
- Subtle glowing line work
- Strong visual focus on the character

The viewer should recognize the collection before recognizing the individual character.

---

# Campaign Exceptions

Campaigns may intentionally deviate from these standards.

Any deviation must be explicitly documented within that campaign's `STYLE.md`.

Campaign exceptions supplement the framework.

They do not modify the framework itself.
